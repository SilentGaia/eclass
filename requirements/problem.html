1.1. Problem Statement

The general problems to be addressed by the Electric Classroom project are the following:

  1. Provide a tool for students to participate, interact, and engage directly with a teacherâ€™s lecture

  2. Provide a way for lectures and notes to seamlessly become a reference, easily accessible by all students
There are a few tools out already which would assist in solving some of these issues, but the main features of the electronic classroom would look at the following areas:

  1. Being able to create layers over the lectures, being able to make slide and ink layers over those on the existing

  2. Being able to give students privileges during a class session, allowing them to participate with editing, questioning or problem solving directly with everybody else

  3. Being able to actively interact not only with the lecture, but every person in the class as well

The Electronic classroom project will address the needs and concerns of a particular customer. This customer needs a suite of features that may be found with a combination of different softwares, but not available as a single package.
