1.5.3. Blackboard Collaboration

Blackboard Collaboration is a virtual classroom that was designed to promote two-way interaction and engagement between users. The main purpose of this tool is provide mobility for students while still having the whole classroom experience by providing an interactive whiteboard, built-in audio and webcam software to communicate with others, and other features to help the whole learning experience.

Good Features:

  • Consist of an audio and video panel to let students and teacher communicate in real-time, letting up to 6 participants with the ability to talk or video simultaneously
  • Option to make video panel follow speakers (user webcam is displayed to who is speaking)
  • Provides a list of logged-in students in session which also displays what each participants are doing
  • Teacher has the control to grant student privileges to interact with all the functions provided
  • Provides chat room that allows teacher and students to interact privately or publicly with the whole classroom
  • Students can also chat with other students individually
  • Teacher has the ability to supervise chat rooms
  • Interactive whiteboard to allow students and teacher to draw or write
  • Teacher can use whiteboard to upload powerpoints for class to see
  • Provides different layers(publicly or privately) for students and teacher to take notes or add onto powerpoint or the interactive whiteboard
  • Able to record sessions and save it for future references

Bad Features:

  • Requires teacher to individually turn off studentsâ€™ ability to use the chat room
  • Everything seems to be crammed onto one screen

Missing Features:

  • Unable to change visibility of layers and ink (no sliders)
  • Unable to give student admin power
  • Does not give the teacher the ability to add lectures manually
  • Does not allow teacher to upload homework assignment for students to easily grab
  • No calendar to let students know when deadlines are
  • Unable to turn off chat room with a single click
  • No sticky note for students or teacher to annotate on
  • No ability to stop students from using other applications
